“Toto… I Don’t Think We’re in Louisiana Anymore”

Welcome to New Mexico — where the creatures look like they escaped from a video game, a comic book, and an accidental science fair experiment. Louisiana has gators, cottonmouths, and mosquitoes the size of small children… but it did not prepare you for this:

🦂 Scorpion

“Sand crawfish.”
Glow under UV like neon aliens. Small. Armored. Irritated at all times.

Where: Everywhere dry, warm, or inconvenient.
Tip: Shake your shoes. Every. Single. Time.

🕷 Tarantula

“An 8-legged Ewok.”
Huge, hairy, and surprisingly polite. Walks like it’s late for book club but doesn’t want trouble.

Where: Southern NM, especially at night.
Tip: Let the fuzzy dorks live their life.

🐗 Javelina

“A pig with PTSD, a gang, and the confidence of a drunk LSU fan.”
Travels in squads. Makes piglet honks. Smells like hot Fritos. Zero sense of personal space.

Where: Roswell southward, Carlsbad, Sitting Bull Falls, desert edges.
Tip: Don’t offer snacks. They’ll take the whole bag and your dignity.

🐍 Rattlesnake

“Desert doorbell.”
Shake-shake-shake = “Hello, please don’t step here.”

They’re polite. Respectful. Just don’t surprise them.

Where: Pretty much everywhere except high mountain zones.
Tip: If you hear maracas in the bushes, don’t go looking for the Mariachi band.

🦎 Horned Lizard

“Dragon nugget.”
Round. Spiky. Drama queens. They puff up like a balloon when nervous.

Where: Open desert & grasslands.
Tip: Observe, don’t scoop. They take themselves VERY seriously.

🦂 Vinegaroon

“Nightmare fuel.”
Looks like a horror movie extra. Acts like a shy introvert. Sprays vinegar mist when upset.

Where: Roswell & Carlsbad region.
Tip: Avoid the mystery vinegar.

🦡 Badger

“Satan’s guard dog.”
Wide. Low. Built like a furry bulldozer with anger management problems.

Where: Desert plains, burrows, anywhere you weren’t expecting one.
Tip: Give it space. Give it respect. Give it distance. Give it the whole desert.

🦨 Desert Skunk

“A stink bomb with racing stripes.”
Cute from the front, catastrophic from the back.

Where: Grasslands, desert edges, trails, and anywhere you wish it wasn’t.
Tip: If the tail goes up, it’s about to go down.

🐇 Jackrabbit

“A rabbit jacked up on stilts and Red Bull.”
Long legs. Turbo mode. Goes from calm to cartoon speed instantly.

Where: Everywhere dry.
Tip: Don’t swerve. They have plot armor.

🦎 Gila Monster

“Poison gilagator.”
Brightly colored, chunky, slow, grumpy… and extremely rare.

Where: Mostly southern NM.
Tip: Protected species. Admire from afar like a celebrity sighting.

🦅 Golden Eagle

“Boss hawk.”
Huge wings. Top-level energy. Will stare into your soul just because it can.

Where: Open desert valleys, canyons, plains.
Tip: Great for photos. Not great for small pets.

🐛 Giant Desert Centipede

“Nope Olympics champion.”
Fast. Brightly colored. Looks like a Subway sandwich with legs.

Where: Beneath logs, rocks, and your nightmares.
Tip: Observe only from the safety of another state.

🦊 Kit Fox

“Cosplay cat.”
Tiny desert ninjas with ears big enough to pick up radio stations.

Where: Desert plains at dusk or dawn.
Tip: Glowing eyes + giant ears = you met a kit fox.

🐏 Bighorn Sheep

“Bam ram.”
Cliff-climbing champions that make gravity look optional.

Where: Taos, Rio Grande Gorge, Enchanted Circle.
Tip: Never bet against a sheep that lives on a cliff.

🦌 Elk

“Muscle deer.”
Massive, majestic, louder than expected.

Where: Valles Caldera, Jemez Mountains, high meadows.
Tip: If it’s bigger than your car, admire from afar.

🐻 Black Bear

“Danger rug.”
Usually shy. Always hungry.

Where: Bandelier & Jemez areas.
Tip: Don’t leave coolers out.

🦌 Mule Deer

“Big ear deer.”
Big ears. Big jumps. Big “Did I leave the oven on?” energy.

Where: Literally everywhere.
Tip: They WILL cross the road at the worst possible time.

🐈 Bobcat

“Leveled-up housecat.”
Fluffy. Fierce. Private.

Where: Forest edges and canyons.
Tip: Rare to spot, unforgettable if you do.

🦅 Raven

“Brain chicken.”
Huge, loud, ridiculously smart, and always judging your life choices.

Where: Everywhere with sky.
Tip: Don’t try to outsmart one.

🐃 Oryx

“Desert unicorn.”
Yes — African antelope in New Mexico.

Where: Southern NM deserts.
Tip: Spot one and you’ve basically entered Narnia.

🐃 Pronghorn

“Turbo deer.”
Second-fastest land animal on Earth.

Where: High plains and grasslands.
Tip: They’ll be in the next zip code before your camera snaps.

🐀 Kangaroo Rat

“Bounce mouse.”
Tiny desert acrobat that launches itself with zero warning.

Where: Desert floors after dark.
Tip: Sudden bouncing in your flashlight beam = Olympic rodent.

🐺 Coyote

“Dog with a sense of humor.”
Smart, sneaky, curious, and always up to something.

Where: Everywhere.
Tip: If it looks like a dog but judges you like a cat — it’s a coyote.

🐺 Mexican Gray Wolf

“Husky husky.”
Rare, majestic, and shy.

Where: Gila Wilderness region.
Tip: Spotting one = wildlife achievement unlocked.

🐦 Roadrunner

“Turbo chicken.”
Fast, fearless, sassy.

Where: Roadsides and desert scrub.
Tip: Blink and it’s gone.

🐾 Prairie Dog

“Whack-a-mole.”
Chirps alarms and dives underground like it’s late for a meeting.

Where: Grasslands north of Santa Fe & near Valles Caldera.
Tip: If one is yelling, it’s yelling at everyone.

⭐ Hoover Family Desert Rules

  • If it rattles → back up.
  • If it glows → don’t touch.
  • If it grunts → let it grunt alone.
  • If it sprays → you’re too close.
