🌌 Night Sky Photography Tips

(Dark desert skies + waning crescent moon = perfect conditions)

1. Stabilize Your Phone
Use a small tripod or prop against a rock, backpack, cooler, or camper roof.
The phone must remain still.
Movement = streaks and blur.

2. Turn Off Flash
You’re capturing ancient light.
Flash ruins it instantly.

3. Enable Night Mode / Long Exposure
Most phones now support:
Night Mode
Star Mode
Long Exposure

4. Let the phone work for you.
Tap to Focus (on a star).
Tap the brightest star and hold to lock focus.

5. Lower Exposure
Slide exposure down slightly. Reduces blown-out sky haze.

6. Do NOT Zoom
Zoom adds noise. Move your body instead.

7. Breathe Control
When you tap the shutter:
Hold your breath
Don’t shift your weight
Don’t touch the tripod

8. Include Foreground
Add:
A silhouette cactus
Car roof
Person standing still
Rock or ridge line

Foreground anchors the photo.

9. Aim Southeast After 10:00 PM
June skies reveal the Milky Way core in the southeast. This is the best direction for galaxy shots.

10. Shoot Satellite Trails
Long exposure will catch:
Straight lines = satellites
Quick, tapered lines = meteors

You’ll capture at least one.

11. Try Silhouette Portraits
Place a lantern behind the subject.
Aim phone at the stars.
Hold still 6–10 seconds.
You get a glowing outline + star field.

12. Manual Settings (If Available)
Shutter: 6–20 sec
ISO: 400–1600
Focus: ∞ (Infinity)
White Balance: 4000–4500K

13. RAW Format (Bonus)
If your phone allows RAW: Use it.
You can brighten shadows without adding noise.


Composition Tips

Horizon on bottom third
Center the Milky Way “river”
Scorpius + Sagittarius look GREAT in frame


Pro Tip

Your camera sees stars your eyes can’t.
